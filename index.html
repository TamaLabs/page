Tama is building neuromorphic systems for immediate humanitarian impact. Our goals are to create autonomous systems to map the tree of life, for disaster response and planetary exploration. In short, we want to create systems that extend human intellectual and physical reach; not replace it. To enable this future, Tama is creating spiking neural networksSpiking Neural Networks (SNNs) are brain-inspired models that communicate using discrete events called 'spikes,' to work This allows them to process temporal data efficiently and operate with significantly less power (about 4 9V batteries). capable of perceiving and interacting with its environment. Our aspirations are to solve problems for underserved communities and create autonomy that works in extreme environments where the human body can’t.
